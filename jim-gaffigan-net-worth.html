Jim Gaffigan: Movies & TV Shows, Book Publications Endorsements

Movies & TV Shows

Besides stand-up comedy, Jim has also had a huge involvement in several movies and tv shows. In fact, he co-created a couple of television shows, too, one even based on his own life.

Initially, he got into acting through commercials. He’s appeared in over 200 commercials for decent companies like Rolling Rock, Saturn, Chrysler, and ESPN.

In 2000, he was cast as a member of The Ellen Show, and in 2001, and also showed face in the popular sitcom That 70’s Show.

He was then cast as a regular member of the show My Boys and appeared in the movies The Love Guru and Mike Myer after a while.

His other notable works include The Jim Gaffigan Show, Troop Zero, Jim Gaffigan: The Pale Tourist, Jim Gaffigan: Obsessed, Being Frank, Comedians in Cars Getting Coffee, and more.

Furthermore, he is also notable for his voice-over works. From voicing an animated version of himself to playing different characters on different shows, he has made quite the name for himself.

His voice-over shows include Bob’s Burgers, Shorty McShorty’s Shorts, WordGirl, Star vs. the Forces of Evil, and the feature Duck Duck Goose.

Book Publications

Gaffigan is also a best-selling published author. His first book, “Dad is Fat,” was released in 2013 and debuted at number 5 on The New York Times Best Seller’s list.

The title was given by his eldest son, who at the time was just 4 or 5 years old. The book is a compilation of essays dealing with the raising of children and the memories of his childhood.

Furthermore, he signed a deal with Crown Publishing in 2013 to write the second book of comic essays. The book “Food: A Love Story” was published in 2014.

Jim Gaffigan: Career

Right after graduating from college, Gaffigan worked as a litigation consultant for a while. In 1990, he moved to New York to pursue a career in comedy.

Gaffigan actually started his career through advertisements. He’s made over 200 of those, and after auditioning several times for “The Late Night Show with David Letterman,” he finally landed a part.

He was successful and well appreciated by the audience, which allowed him to appear in a letterman-owned production company, World Wide Pants.

After that, he appeared in a couple of booming sitcoms, including That 70’s Show, Sex and the City, Third Watch, Ed, and Law & Order. 

Following that, he slowly returned back to stand-up and, in time-released over ten comedy albums including The Pale Tourist, Obsessed, Beyond the Pale, Noble Ape, and more.

He even had his own show, called “The Jim Gaffigan Show” at one point. Likewise, he’s also known for his voice-acting roles and the 2 books he has authored.
