The KISS Concert in Knoxville for 2023 was canceled due to Paul Stanley's flu illness, leading to show cancellations in Ottawa and Toronto as well.

Is the KISS Concert in Knoxville Cancelled 2023?

Yes, the KISS concert in Knoxville, scheduled as part of their "End of the Road Tour," has been canceled in 2023. Unfortunately, one of the band members, Paul Stanley, fell ill with the flu, leading to the cancellation of not only the Knoxville show but also their performances in Ottawa and Toronto. The announcement was made on social media, with the venue, Thompson-Boling Arena at Food City Center, confirming the cancellation just hours before the scheduled performance.

Refunds are being offered to ticket-holders at their point of purchase, and there's currently no information on whether the show will be rescheduled. This marks the third cancellation or postponement of a major band's show at Thompson-Boling Arena due to illness in the same year, following similar incidents with The Chicks and Aerosmith.

Despite the setbacks, KISS is still scheduled to perform their "final show ever" at Madison Square Garden in New York on December 2, concluding their extensive farewell tour that began in January 2019 but was briefly halted in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Why was the KISS Concert Canceled?

The KISS concert in Knoxville was canceled because frontman Paul Stanley was battling the flu, making it impossible for the band to perform. This unexpected illness forced the cancellation not only of the Knoxville show but also affected their performances in Ottawa and Toronto earlier in the week. The announcement, made on social media just hours before the scheduled concert, expressed the band's deep disappointment and explained that they had done everything possible to proceed with the shows despite Stanley's flu.

With the cancellation, fans were assured that refunds would be available at the point of purchase, and while there is no mention of rescheduling, KISS is still set to conclude their "End of the Road Tour" with a final show at Madison Square Garden in New York on December 2.
