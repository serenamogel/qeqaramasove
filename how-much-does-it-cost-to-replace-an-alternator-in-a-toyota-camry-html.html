What is the root cause of an alternator failure?

Diodes that have failed are a typical cause of alternator failure. The diodes are a component of the rectifier assembly, which transforms the alternator’s alternating current output to direct current. It is also possible for current to drain from the battery via the alternator while the car is not being driven because of a faulty diode.
